PROJECT ANNAPURNA

Empowering Acid Attack Survivors and Marginalized Communities

Motive

Project Annapurna is a powerful social entrepreneurship initiative designed to make a meaningful impact on the lives of acid attack survivors and marginalized communities. The project's core motive is to provide employment opportunities and support for acid attack survivors, helping them regain their confidence and independence.

Implementation

At the heart of this initiative is "Beyond the Scars," a campus café that serves as a tribute to these resilient individuals. The café not only offers a space for survivors to work and build a future but also aims to foster awareness and empathy within the college community. By supporting Project Annapurna, we strive to create a more inclusive and supportive environment, where social entrepreneurship paves the way for positive change and empowerment.

Impact Created

Project Annapurna significantly impacts by empowering acid attack survivors through employment at the "Beyond the Scars" café, helping them regain independence and rebuild their lives. It raises awareness about their challenges, provides valuable skill development, and supports NGOs. The project also offers students practical experience through internships, promoting social entrepreneurship and fostering a compassionate, inclusive campus environment.
